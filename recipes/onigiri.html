Onigiri Recipe

Description

Onigiri are traditional Japanese rice balls typically shaped into triangles or rounds and filled with savory ingredients like umeboshi, tuna, or salmon, often wrapped in nori seaweed for a portable, satisfying snack. They are a staple in bento boxes and are enjoyed for their convenience and flavor.

Ingredients

  • Japanese Short-grain White Rice
  • Water
  • Diamond Crystal Kosher Salt
  • Dried Nori
  • Umeboshi
  • Canned Tuna
  • Kewpie Mayo
  • Kikkoman Soy Sauce
  • Dried Bonito Flakes
  • White Roasted Sesame Seeds
  • Furikake

Steps

  1. Measure 2¼ cups of uncooked Japanese short-grain rice and rinse it thoroughly under cool water until the water runs clear, using a fine-mesh strainer.
  2. Cook the rinsed rice with 2½ cups of water in a rice cooker or saucepan, following standard instructions for Japanese rice (typically a 1:1.1 to 1:1.2 rice-to-water ratio). Allow the rice to rest for 10 minutes after cooking.
  3. While the rice cooks, prepare fillings: mix tuna with Kewpie mayonnaise and 1 tsp soy sauce; combine bonito flakes with 2 tsp soy sauce to make a damp mixture.
  4. Set up an assembly line: place fillings on a rimmed baking sheet, cover the work surface with plastic wrap, and have a bowl of warm water (with ½ tsp salt dissolved) ready for wetting hands.
  5. Wet your hands in the salted water, then scoop about 1/3 cup (65 g) of warm rice onto the plastic wrap.
  6. Gently shape the rice into a triangle using your palms, then make a small indentation in the center.
  7. Add 1–2 tsp of filling (tuna mayo or bonito soy sauce) into the indentation, then cover with more rice and press firmly to seal.
  8. Shape the rice ball into a triangle by pressing between your hands, ensuring it holds its form.
  9. Wrap the onigiri with a 1- by 4-inch strip of nori, pressing lightly to adhere.
  10. Coat the outside with furikake seasoning by dipping each side into the mixture.
  11. Place finished onigiri on a tray covered with a damp cloth to prevent drying.
