Arepa Recipe

Description

Arepas are traditional flatbreads from Colombia and Venezuela, made from pre-cooked cornmeal, naturally gluten-free and often vegan. They are versatile, crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, and can be served plain or stuffed with a variety of fillings.

Ingredients

  • Pre-cooked cornmeal (masarepa or P.A.N. flour)
  • Salt
  • Warm Water
  • Vegetable Oil or Butter
  • Optional fillings: Cheese, avocado, black beans, scrambled eggs, or your favorite savory ingredients

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, combine the warm water and salt, stirring until dissolved. Gradually add the pre-cooked cornmeal while mixing with your hands or a spoon until a soft, moist, and malleable dough forms. The dough should be tacky but not sticky; if too dry, add water 1 tablespoon at a time; if too sticky, add a small amount of cornmeal.
  2. Cover the bowl with a towel or plastic wrap and let the dough rest for at least 5–10 minutes to hydrate.
  3. Divide the dough into 8 equal portions (about 1/4 to 1/3 cup each). Roll each portion into a ball using moist hands. Flatten each ball into a disk about 3–4 inches (7.5–10 cm) in diameter and 1/4 inch (0.6 cm) thick, ensuring smooth edges.
  4. Heat a skillet or griddle over medium-high heat. Lightly oil the surface with vegetable oil or butter. Add the arepas in batches and cook for 5–7 minutes per side, or until golden brown with a dry, slightly charred crust. For a crispier texture, cook longer; for a softer interior, reduce time slightly.
  5. Transfer cooked arepas to a wire rack or paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  6. Once cool enough to handle, use a sharp knife to slice each arepa horizontally halfway through, creating a pocket. Fill with cheese, avocado, beans, or other desired fillings.
